About Gait and Parkinson's Disease

Freezing of gait (FOG) is a brief, episodic absence or marked reduction of forward progression of the feet despite the intention to walk. It is common in Parkinson's disease (PD), particularly in advanced stages, and is linked with falls and reduced quality of life.

  • Common triggers: turning, doorways, dual-tasking, start hesitation.
  • Key observable features: increased step time variability, reduced stride length, asymmetry, and prolonged turns.
  • This tool provides a heuristic screening based on simple thresholds and is not a diagnostic device.

For the ML system in this project, wearable accelerometer data (vertical, medio-lateral, anterior–posterior axes) are used to detect FOG-related events with deep learning models. Evaluation uses cross-validation and ensembling.
